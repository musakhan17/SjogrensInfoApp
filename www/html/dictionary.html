Dictionary

Some complex terms for you.


Sjogren Syndrome

  • An immune system disorder characterized by dry eyes and dry mouth.
  • Named after Henrik Sjögren, a swedish ophthalmologist.
  • It is a chronic disease that can last for years or be lifelong
  • 1 to 4 million people in the U.S. have Sjögren’s syndrome
Amyloidosis - The buildup of amyloid proteins in the heart, kidneys, liver, or other organs.

Blepharitis - An inflammation of the eyelid that affects the eyelashes or tear production.

Lupus - An inflammatory disease caused when the immune system attacks its own tissues.

Ophthalmologist - A specialist in the branch of medicine concerned with the study and treatment of disorders and diseases of the eye.

Otorhinolaryngology - Subspecialists that’s main focus is on the ears, nose and throat and related above the neck.

Parkinson’s disease - A disorder of the central nervous system that affects movement, often including tremors.

Rheumatoid Arthritis - A chronic inflammatory disorder affecting many joints, including those in the hands and feet.

Scleroderma - Chronic hardening and tightening of the skin and connective tissues.

Xerostomia - It is defined as dry mouth resulting from reduced or absent saliva flow.
